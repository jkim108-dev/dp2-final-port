East Asian Painting

Asian Painting Series

This work originated from the emotion of anxiety. When I was living in Korea, I often had a habit of climbing the mountain behind my house whenever I felt worried or emotionally overwhelmed, using nature as a way to calm my mind and regain stability.

While studying in the United States, I began to connect the various anxieties and pressures of adapting to a new environment with this personal habit from Korea. This project was created as a visual exploration of that emotional connection, reflecting both psychological tension and the sense of comfort and balance found in nature.

Atmosphere Study

Cloud Series

This work began from the feeling of burnout. Coming to the United States for the first time, I often experienced burnout due to unfamiliar language, culture, and the challenges of adapting to a completely new environment.

Whenever I felt exhausted or discouraged, I would spend time on LinkedIn looking through other people’s careers and projects, imagining my own future and regaining motivation through their journeys.

Because of this experience, LinkedIn gradually came to feel comforting to me, almost like soft clouds that offered emotional support and hope. Inspired by that personal feeling, I expressed the idea through cloud-like forms, translating emotional comfort and aspiration into a visual and physical representation.
